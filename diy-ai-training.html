DIY-AI Training

In-person AI training for small business teams across central and western Colorado. The basics, taught in plain language.

What this is

I come to your office and spend time with your team learning the fundamentals of working with AI — the same fundamentals I have been working out in my own practice for the last year and a half. The goal is for your people to leave with usable skills they can apply to the work in front of them on Monday morning, and a shared vocabulary that compounds across everything you do after.

What we cover

Foundational AI literacy.

What these tools are, what they do, and where they break. Enough grounding for the rest of the day to make sense.

Prompt engineering basics.

How to talk to an AI tool so you get useful output. The handful of patterns that account for most of the difference.

Capturing workflows as repeatable skills.

Turning your team's recurring time-eaters into repeatable AI patterns, using examples from your actual work.

Planning sessions vs. execution sessions.

Why keeping the thinking and the doing in separate AI sessions produces better work in both — and how to set it up.

A shared team vocabulary.

When your team leaves with shared terms for the work, every future conversation about AI gets cheaper. This is where adoption usually stalls.

Who this is for

Small business teams of up to twenty people. Smaller is fine — some of the best sessions are with three or four people in a room. The fit is best when there is at least one person on the team who wants this to land and is willing to keep using what we cover after I leave.

The reason I think this is worth your time is that I am close enough to the learning curve to remember what it felt like at the bottom — and I am far enough up it now to explain the parts that confused me to people who are about to confuse themselves the same way. That is the work.

Where I work

I am based in Salida and travel to teams across central and western Colorado — roughly a two-hour drive in any direction. The named counties are Chaffee, Eagle, Lake, Summit, Pitkin, Fremont, Clear Creek, and Saguache, with surrounding counties in the Colorado Springs to Gunnison corridor regularly within reach.

Starting at $1,000 for a day on-site, plus mileage from Salida. Multi-session and follow-up arrangements are scoped per engagement.
